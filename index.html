Chair Rusty Hicks — Authority, Constraints & Coalition Power

Legitimate power flows from the Chair's formal title and bylaw-granted authority. It operates downward through CADEM's hierarchy — committee appointments, convention chairship, endorsing caucus designation, and agenda control.
Reward & Coercive power — internally, the Chair rewards through committee appointments and process timing; coercive leverage is indirect. Externally, this power is nearly absent — the Chair cannot compel candidates, donors, or PACs.
Referent power derives from Hicks' personal relationships and labor movement credibility (UFCW). It flows outward toward electeds, activists, and press — entities the bylaws give him no formal authority over.
Expert power comes from Hicks' knowledge of California political geography, coalition dynamics, and electoral mechanics. Candidates and legislative leaders seek his read on races even when they don't need his formal approval.
